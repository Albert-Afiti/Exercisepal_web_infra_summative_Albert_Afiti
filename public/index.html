About
DrugsPal

A free, open-access drug information platform built for patients, caregivers, and healthcare students.

DRUG SEARCH

Look up any prescription or OTC medication by brand or generic name. Results include purpose, dosage instructions, warnings, side effects, and contraindications — all sourced directly from FDA-approved drug labels.

Interaction Checker

Enter 2–5 medications to check for known drug-drug interactions. Each interaction is classified by severity (High, Moderate, Low) using the NIH RxNorm interaction database, helping you spot potentially dangerous combinations.

RECALL ALERTS

Search for FDA enforcement actions and recall notices for any medication. Results include recall classification (Class I–III), the reason for recall, current status, and the date the recall was initiated.

MY MEDICINE CABINET

Save frequently used or prescribed medications to your personal cabinet for quick access. Your cabinet is stored privately in your browser — nothing is sent to any server. From the cabinet, jump directly to search details, interactions, or recall checks.

DATA SOURCES

FDA
OpenFDA Drug Label API

Drug information (purpose, dosage, warnings, side effects) comes from the FDA's official structured product labeling database, updated continuously with the latest approved labels.

NIH
RxNorm / RxNav (NLM)

Drug identifiers (RxCUI) and interaction data come from the National Library of Medicine's RxNorm database, the standard for clinical drug nomenclature in the United States.

FDA
OpenFDA Drug Enforcement API

Recall and enforcement data comes from the FDA's drug enforcement reporting system, which tracks voluntary and mandatory product recalls and market withdrawals.

How to Use

  1. Drug Search: Type a medication name (brand or generic) and press Search. Click any section card to expand detailed information. Use the sort buttons to reorder sections.
  2. Interaction Checker: Type each drug name and press Enter to add it as a tag. Add 2–5 drugs, then click Check. Use the severity filter buttons to focus on high-risk interactions.
  3. Recall Alerts: Enter a drug name and click Check Recalls. Filter results by recall class (I = most serious) or search by keyword.
  4. Medicine Cabinet: After searching a drug, click "Save to Cabinet" to store it. Access your saved medications any time from the Cabinet tab and quickly jump to search, interactions, or recalls.

Privacy

DrugsPal does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Your Medicine Cabinet is saved exclusively in your browser's local storage and never leaves your device. Search queries are passed to the FDA and NIH APIs for data retrieval but are not logged by DrugsPal.
